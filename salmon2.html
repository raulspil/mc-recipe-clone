Easy Sweet & Sour Salmon Traybake

You’ll love this easy traybake dinner, which only takes 10 minutes to prep. You’ll coat responsibly‑sourced salmon and vibrant pak choi with a homemade sweet & sour sauce. Brown rice, crunchy mangetout and sesame seeds complete the dish.

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 35 mins Total: 45 mins Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 carrot
  • 25g honey
  • 160g mangetout
  • 1 onion
  • 2 pak choi
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 4 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 140g tinned pineapple
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 200g brown rice

Cooking Instructions

Before you start

Preheat the oven to 240°C (fan 220°C/gas 9). Bring a large saucepan of salted water to the boil. Pull out a measuring jug, a sieve, a large baking dish & a small mixing bowl.

Get the rice on

Add the rice to the saucepan and boil for 25–30 minutes, until cooked, then drain.

Roast the salmon

Cut the onion into wedges. Cut the carrots into 1 cm half‑moons. Cut the pak choi into bite‑sized pieces. Drain the pineapple, reserving 100 ml of the juice.

Add the onion, carrots and pineapple chunks to the baking dish (≈35 cm). Drizzle with 2 tsp oil, season, toss. Lay the salmon skin‑side down, drizzle 1 tsp oil, season. Bake for 15 minutes.

Meanwhile, make the sweet & sour sauce

Mix pineapple juice, tomato ketchup, tamari, cornflour, honey and rice wine vinegar together.

Last bits

After 15 mins, coat salmon & veg in sauce. Return for 5 mins.

Add mangetout & pak choi, return for 3 mins or until sauce thickens.

Plate up

Check salmon is cooked: it should flake easily. Serve rice topped with salmon & veg, sprinkle sesame seeds.
