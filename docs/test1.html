Creamy Coconut Butterbeans with Sweet Potato and 'Live' Raita

Co-created with Dr Megan Rossi, leading gut health scientist and registered dietitian, this comforting bowl of coconutty beans packs in 50% of your daily fibre intake. You’ll roast vibrant sweet potatoes till golden and delicious, while simmering chunky butterbeans in a warm curry, featuring rich coconut milk, creamy peanut butter and a blend of fragrant spices. Drizzle generously with a homemade ‘live’ raita, made with Bio&Me’s natural & creamy kefir (containing 18 different culture strains) for your gut microbes.


Ingredients

  • 40g creamed coconut
  • 1 midi cucumber
  • 1 handful of fresh mint
  • 60g deep roast peanut butter
  • 160g baby spinach
  • 1 sweet potato
  • 8 tsp ginger & garlic paste
  • 4 tsp garam masala
  • 460g butter beans
  • 4 tbsp tomato puree
  • 200g brown rice
  • 250ml natural kefir

Serving Size

4 people

Cook Time

30 minutes

Cooking Instructions

Before you start
  • Preheat the oven to 220C / fan 200C / gas 7 Line a baking tray with parchment paper Bring a large saucepan filled with salted water to a boil Pull out another large saucepan, a medium bowl, a sieve, a peeler & a measuring jug
  • Boil & roast
    • Cut the sweet potato into 2cm cubes (peel optional). Place onto the lined baking tray and toss with 2 tsp oil and a pinch of salt and pepper. Roast for 25-30 mins, until soft and golden
    • Add the rice to the saucepan of boiling water and boil for 25-30 mins, until cooked, then drain
  • Make the raita
    • Meanwhile, finely dice the cucumber. Pick the mint leaves from the stalks and finely chop
    • Combine the cucumber, mint and half the kefir in the medium bowl. Season with salt, then set aside
    • Drain and rinse the beans
  • Simmer the beans
    • Heat the other large saucepan with 2 tsp oil on medium heat. Once hot, add the beans, garam masala, tomato puree and ginger & garlic paste. Cook for 1 min, until fragrant
    • Stir in the creamed coconut, peanut butter and 400ml water. Bring to the boil and simmer for 4-5 mins, until the coconut has dissolved and the sauce has thickened. Season with salt and pepper
  • Finishing touches
    • Rinse the spinach
    • When ready, stir the roasted sweet potato and spinach into the beans and simmer for 1 min, until wilted
    • Remove from the heat and stir through the remaining kefir. Taste and season with salt and pepper if needed
  • Plate up
    • Serve the rice and creamy butterbeans into bowls
    • Spoon over the raita
