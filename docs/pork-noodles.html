Easy Free-range Chinese Five-spice Pork Noodles

Here’s a quick and easy weeknight meal, inspired by Chinese flavours. Sizzle free-range British pork mince with garlic, spring onions, five-spice and a pinch of chilli. Toss in brown rice noodles, peppers and pak choi for veggies. Then get your chopsticks stuck in!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 3 garlic clove
  • 25g honey
  • 4 spring onion
  • 2 pak choi
  • 2 pepper
  • 500g free-range pork mince
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp Chinese five-spice
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 200g brown rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp tamari

Serving Size

4 people

Prep Time

Cook Time

25 minutes

Cooking Instructions

Before you start
  • Pull out a large saucepan, a large frying pan, a small bowl & a sieve
  • Make the sauce & prep the veg
    • Add the tamari, honey, sesame oil and vinegar to the small bowl. Mix well and set aside
    • Thinly slice the peppers and spring onions. Finely chop or crush the garlic
    • Trim the pak choi, then cut in half lengthways, separate the leaves and chop into thirds
  • Build the dish
    • Heat the large frying pan with 1 tbsp oil on medium-high heat. Once hot, add the pork and cook for 5 mins, until golden brown
    • Add the garlic, five-spice, half the spring onion and half the chilli flakes (to taste)
    • Add the pepper to the pan. Cook for 5 mins, until softening
    • Add the pak choi and sauce. Cook for 3-5 mins, until the sauce thickens and coats the pork
  • Time to noodle
    • Meanwhile, bring the large saucepan filled with salted hot water to a boil
    • Add the noodles, gently separate with a fork and boil for 3-4 mins, until cooked. Drain, then rinse under cold water
    • Add the cooked noodles to the pork pan and toss to combine and heat through. Add a splash of water if it gets too dry
  • Plate up
    • Serve the pork noodles into bowls, sprinkled with the remaining chilli flakes (to taste) and spring onion
