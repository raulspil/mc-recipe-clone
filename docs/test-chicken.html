Honey chicken with stir-fried rice

Sticky sweetness from honey, crunch from sesame seeds – and warmth from five-spice – our Chinese-inspired sauce deliciously coats tender free-range British chicken. Our quick fried rice is just as delicious, studded with sweetcorn and crunchy red pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 head of broccoli
  • ½ tsp chinese five-spice
  • 600g diced British chicken breast
  • 25g honey
  • 4 spring onion
  • 2 pepper
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 20g white sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 140g sweetcorn (drained)
  • 2 tbsp tamari
  • 160g brown rice

Serving Size

Prep Time

Cook Time

30 minutes

Cooking Instructions

  1. Meanwhile, heat a separate large pan with 1 tbsp oil on a medium-high heat. Cook the chicken for 6 mins, turning occasionally. Season, then add the sauce with 50ml water. Cook for a further 2 mins to reduce slightly. Check the chicken is cooked through; cut a large piece in half, the flesh should be white and the juices running clear. Make the sauce. In a small bowl mix together the tamari, honey, vinegar, tomato puree and five-spice. Heat the pan used earlier with 1 tbsp oil on a medium-high heat and cook the peppers and sweetcorn for 5 mins, until softening. Add the cooked rice and mix thoroughly. Season with sea salt and black pepper; cover to keep warm. Boil a kettle. Rinse the rice, then add to a saucepan with 800ml boiling water and a pinch of sea salt. Simmer for 20-25 mins, then drain. Set aside the rice; reuse the pan for the broccoli later. Cut the peppers into bite-sized pieces. Cut the broccoli into florets. Thinly slice the spring onions. Drain the sweetcorn. Toast the sesame seeds in a large, non-stick dry pan on a medium heat, stirring constantly, until evenly golden. Transfer to a small bowl and set aside. Keep the pan for later. Serve the fried rice, broccoli and chicken garnished with the spring onions and sesame seeds. Simmer the broccoli in a pot of lightly salted boiling water for 2 mins or until it is cooked but still retaining a slight bite. Drain; season with sea salt and black pepper.
