Easy Sweet & Sour Salmon Traybake

You'll love this easy traybake dinner, which only takes 10 minutes to prep. You'll coat responsibly-sourced salmon and vibrant pak choi with a homemade sweet & sour sauce. Brown rice, crunchy mangetout and sesame seeds complete the dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 carrot
  • 25g honey
  • 160g mangetout
  • 1 onion
  • 2 pak choi
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 tbsp tamari
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 4 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 140g tinned pineapple
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 200g brown rice

Serving Size

Prep Time

Cook Time

35 minutes

Cooking Instructions

  1. Once the salmon has been roasting for 15 mins, coat the salmon and veg with the sweet & sour sauce Return to the oven for a further 5 mins, until the salmon is cooked through After 5 mins, remove the salmon and add the mangetout and pak choi. Return to the oven for another 3 mins, or until the sauce has thickened Cut the onion into wedges. Cut the carrots into 1cm half-moons. Cut the pak choi into bite-sized pieces. Drain the pineapple, reserving 100ml of the juice Add the onion, carrots and pineapple chunks to the large baking dish (we used a 35cm baking dish). Drizzle with 2 tsp oil and season with salt and pepper. Toss to coat. Lay the salmon on top, skin-side down, drizzle with 1 tsp and season. Bake for 15 mins Add the pineapple juice, ketchup, tamari, cornflour, honey and vinegar to the small mixing bowl and mix well Check your fish is cooked through; it should be steaming hot, opaque and separate easily with a fork Serve the rice topped with the salmon and veg. Sprinkle over the sesame seeds Add the rice to the saucepan and boil for 25-30 mins, until cooked, then drain
