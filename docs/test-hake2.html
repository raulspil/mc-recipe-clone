Mexican-spiced Hake with Black Bean Rice & Avo Salsa

Punchy, Mexican flavours (think: smoked paprika, cumin, coriander and lime) meet our delicate, sustainably-sourced hake. Serve with a mix of juicy sweetcorn, black beans & rice. A chunky avocado and tomato salsa on the side. Perfecto!

Ingredients

  • 1 avocado
  • 1 handful of fresh coriander
  • 2 garlic clove
  • 2 hake fillets (skin off)
  • 1 lime
  • 2 spring onion
  • 80g brown rice
  • 1 tbsp sundried tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Mexican spice mix
  • 230g black beans
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 140g sweetcorn
  • 110g seasonal tomatoes

Serving Size

Prep Time

Cook Time

35 minutes

Cooking Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 240C / fan 220C / gas mark 9. Bring a large saucepan filled with salted hot water to a boil. Add the rice and boil for 25-30 mins, until cooked, then drain.
  2. Make the avo salsa; halve the tomatoes. Roughly chop the coriander. Dice the avocado into 2cm cubes. Toss all in a small mixing bowl, along with the juice of the lime (to taste). Season well with sea salt and black pepper.
  3. Make the marinade; in a large mixing bowl, mix the sundried tomato paste, Mexican spice mix, 1 tsp oil and 2 tbsp water (1 tbsp for one person). Season with sea salt and black pepper, then add the hake fillets and gently toss to coat evenly. Place into an ovenproof dish and roast for 8-10 mins, until cooked through.
  4. Meanwhile, drain the sweetcorn and black beans. Finely chop or crush the garlic. Heat a large frying pan with 1 tsp oil over medium-high heat. Add the sweetcorn, cumin and garlic, and cook for 3-4 mins until beginning to char. Once the rice is cooked, add to the pan along with the black beans. Mix well and cook for 2-3 mins, until warmed through. Season with sea salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Thinly slice the spring onions. Serve the black bean rice with the avo salsa and hake. Spoon over any juices from the fish roasting tray and scatter with the spring onions.
